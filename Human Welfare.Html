Human Welfare

Human welfare refers to the well-being, health, and overall quality of life of individuals and communities. It encompasses a broad range of factors that contribute to the physical, mental, and social flourishing of people. The concept of human welfare emphasizes the need to address fundamental human needs, ensure social justice, and promote the equitable distribution of resources and opportunities. In this context, various domains come into play, including healthcare, education, social services, environmental sustainability, and economic prosperity.

Efforts to enhance human welfare aim to create a society where every person has access to the basic necessities of life, such as food, clean water, shelter, and healthcare, as well as the opportunity to reach their full potential through education, employment, and a supportive social environment. This introductory concept underscores the importance of policies, initiatives, and actions that prioritize the well-being of individuals and society as a whole. By fostering human welfare, we can work towards a more just, equitable, and compassionate world where everyone has the chance to lead a meaningful and fulfilling life. Access to healthcare, for instance, is a crucial component of human welfare. It ensures that individuals can receive medical treatment and preventive care, leading to healthier, longer, and more productive lives. Similarly, access to education empowers people with the knowledge and skills they need to participate fully in society and the economy.


There are numerous organizations around the world dedicated to promoting and improving human welfare in various ways. These organizations work on a wide range of issues, including healthcare, education, poverty alleviation, human rights, and environmental sustainability. Here are some well-known international organizations that focus on human welfare:

  1. World Food Programme (WFP):

    It was Established in 1961. The WFP is the food assistance branch of the United Nations. It aims to eradicate hunger and improve food security around the world by providing emergency food aid and supporting long-term solutions to hunger.

  2. Amnesty International:

    It was Founded on May 28, 1961. This human rights organization works to combat human rights abuses and advocates for justice and accountability for violations of human rights.

  3. Oxfam:

    It was Founded in 1942. Oxfam is a confederation of organizations working to reduce poverty and inequality by addressing issues related to economic development, disaster response, and social justice.

  4. The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation:

    It was Established in January 2000. This private foundation is dedicated to addressing global health, poverty, and education challenges. It works to improve healthcare, reduce poverty, and enhance access to education.

  5. International Labour Organization (ILO):

    It was Established on April 11, 1919.The ILO, a specialized agency of the United Nations, focuses on labor issues and workers' rights. It promotes decent work, fair labor standards, and social protection.

Here are some well-known human welfare organizations in India:

  1. SEWA (Self Employed Women's Association) :

    SEWA was founded in 1972. SEWA is a trade union and organization that focuses on the welfare and rights of women working in the informal sector.

  2. CRY (Child Rights and You) :

    CRY was established in 1979.CRY is dedicated to advocating for and protecting the rights of underprivileged children in India.

  3. Pratham :

    Pratham is one of India's largest non:governmental organizations working to improve the quality of education for underprivileged children.

  4. HelpAge India :

    HelpAge India has been operational in India since 1978. HelpAge India is an organization that focuses on the welfare of senior citizens in India, providing support for the elderly.

  5. Goonj :

    This organization began its operations in 1999. Goonj is known for its disaster relief and rural development initiatives, addressing clothing and other basic needs in underprivileged communities.

  6. Akshaya Patra :

    Akshaya Patra began its meal program in 2000. Akshaya Patra is an organization that provides mid day meals to school children, aiming to end classroom hunger and promote education.

  7. Smile Foundation :

    Smile Foundation was established in 2002.Smile Foundation works on a variety of social issues, including education, healthcare, and livelihood support for underprivileged children and their families.

  8. Nanhi Kali :

    The Nanhi Kali program started in the 1990s as an initiative of the K. C. Mahindra Education Trust.. Nanhi Kali is an initiative that focuses on supporting the education of underprivileged girls in India.

  9. SOS Children's Village India :

    SOS Children's Village has been operating in India since the 1960s. SOS Children's Village India provides a family like environment for orphaned and abandoned children.

Here are some important human welfare programs in India

  1. National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA):

    Launched on February 2, 2006, this program guarantees 100 days of wage employment to every rural household.

  2. Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Campaign):

    Launched on October 2, 2014, this initiative aims to improve sanitation and eliminate open defecation.

  3. Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY):

    Launched on August 28, 2014, this program focuses on financial inclusion by providing access to banking services for all.

  4. Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY):

    Launched on May 9, 2015, this is an accident insurance scheme that offers affordable insurance coverage.

  5. Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY):

    Launched on May 9, 2015, this is a life insurance scheme that provides coverage at a low premium.

  6. Digital India:

    Launched on July 1, 2015, this program focuses on promoting digitalization and technology adoption to improve service delivery and digital infrastructure.

  7. Atal Pension Yojana (APY):

    Launched on May 9, 2015, this program aims to provide pension benefits to the unorganized sector.

  8. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana:

    Launched on January 22, 2015, this scheme encourages savings for the girl child's education and marriage.

  9. National Nutrition Mission (POSHAN Abhiyan):

    Launched on March 8, 2018, this program focuses on improving the nutritional status of children and pregnant women.

  10. Ujjwala Yojana:

    Launched on May 1, 2016, this scheme provides free LPG connections to women from below-poverty-line households, promoting clean cooking and reducing indoor air pollution.

  11. Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN):

    Launched on February 24, 2019, this initiative provides financial support to small and marginal farmers through direct income transfers.
