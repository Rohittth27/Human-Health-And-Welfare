Family Welfare

Family welfare refers to a comprehensive approach aimed at improving the well-being and quality of life of families and individuals within a society. It encompasses a wide range of programs, policies, and initiatives designed to promote the physical, emotional, social, and economic health of families. Family welfare initiatives often focus on issues such as family planning, maternal and child health, education, economic support, and social services to strengthen the family unit.

The primary goals of family welfare are to create an environment in which families can thrive and fulfill their potential, as well as to address various challenges that families may encounter, such as poverty, healthcare access, education, and social support. By providing resources and support to families, these programs aim to enhance the overall well-being and stability of society. Family welfare programs and policies vary from one country to another, reflecting the unique cultural, social, and economic contexts in which they operate. They often involve a collaboration between government agencies, non-governmental organizations, and community groups to ensure that families receive the necessary assistance and support to lead fulfilling lives.


Ultimately, the concept of family welfare underscores the importance of strong and healthy families as a foundation for a prosperous and harmonious society. It acknowledges that the well-being of individuals is closely tied to the well-being of their families, and by extension, the well-being of society as a whole.


India has launched several family welfare programs over the years to address various aspects of family well-being, including healthcare, education, nutrition, and social support. Here are some notable family welfare programs in India:

  1. Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK):

    Launched in February 2013, RBSK focuses on child health and early detection of birth defects, developmental delays, and disabilities in children.

  2. Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY):

    Launched in January 2017, PMMVY is a maternity benefit program that provides financial assistance to pregnant and lactating women.

  3. Rajiv Gandhi National Creche Scheme for the Children of Working Mothers:

    This scheme was launched in October 2006 and provides daycare facilities and early childhood education to children of working mothers.

  4. Indira Gandhi Matritva Sahyog Yojana (IGMSY):

    Launched in 2010, this program provides cash incentives to pregnant and lactating women to improve maternal and child health and promote better nutrition.

  5. National Family Planning Program:

    Launched in 1952, this was one of the earliest family welfare programs in India. It aimed to control the rapid growth of the population by promoting family planning methods and contraception.

  6. Mission Parivar Vikas:

    This initiative focuses on improving access to family planning services and promoting family planning methods, aiming to control population growth.

  7. Balika Samriddhi Yojana:

    Launched in 1997, this scheme focuses on the overall welfare and development of the girl child, promoting their education and health.

  8. Rashtriya Mahila Kosh (RMK):

    RMK, established in 1993, provides micro-credit and financial services to empower women and improve their socio-economic status.

  9. Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA):

    Initiated in 2016, this program offers free antenatal care check-ups to pregnant women on the 9th of every month, ensuring the well-being of expectant mothers.

  10. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana:

    This savings scheme, launched in 2015, encourages parents to save for the future of their girl child, promoting long-term financial security.
