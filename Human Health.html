Human Health

Human health is a multifaceted and invaluable aspect of our lives that encompasses physical, mental, and social well-being. It is the foundation upon which we build our experiences, dreams, and aspirations. The state of human health is a dynamic interplay of genetics, lifestyle, environment, and access to healthcare services. It is not merely the absence of disease but the presence of vitality, resilience, and a sense of fulfillment.

Maintaining and enhancing human health is a universal goal, as it enables us to lead productive lives, form meaningful relationships, and pursue our passions. It is a delicate balance that requires attention to nutrition, exercise, mental well-being, and the fostering of a supportive community. In an ever-changing world, human health is an evolving conversation, continually influenced by advances in science, technology, and societal awareness.

Health is affected by:

  • Genetic disorders - heritable defects of parents to offspring.
  • Infections
  • Life style including food and water we take, rest and exercise we give to our bodies, habits that we have or lack etc.

Disease is a broad term used to describe a deviation from normal functioning in the body or an organ system, resulting in physiological or psychological dysfunction. It encompasses a wide range of conditions, disorders, and illnesses that can affect the health and well-being of an individual.Diseases can be broadly grouped into infectious and non-infectious.

Infectious

Diseases which are easily transmitted from one person to another through various modes, including direct contact, airborne particles, contaminated food or water, and insect vectors are called infectious diseases.Infectious diseases have played a pivotal role throughout human history, shaping societies and driving medical advancements, but they continue to pose a significant global health threat.

Non Infectious

Diseases Which are not easily transmitted from one person to another is called non infectious diseases.These diseases often develop over an extended period and are primarily associated with lifestyle, genetics, environmental factors, and aging. Non-infectious diseases include conditions like heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and chronic respiratory disorders. They are a leading cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide.

Here are a few international organizations dedicated to human health:

World Health Organization (WHO)

Established on April 7, 1948, by the United Nations. It is the primary global organization for health.

United Nations Children's Fund

Founded on December 11, 1946, to provide humanitarian and developmental assistance to children and mothers around the world.

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

Founded on July 1, 1946, it plays a crucial role in public health and disease prevention in the United States and globally.

Médecins Sans Frontières

Founded in 1971, this organization provides medical care in areas affected by crises, such as armed conflicts and epidemics.

Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance

Founded in 2000, Gavi focuses on increasing access to immunization in low-income countries, contributing to public health and the prevention of deadly diseases.

World Heart Federation (WHF)

The World Heart Federation is a global organization that was established to combat cardiovascular diseases and promote heart health. It was founded in 1970.

Here are some key human health programs in India:

Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana

National health insurance program that was launched in 2018. It provides financial protection for hospitalization expenses for over 100 million families in India.

National Rural Health Mission

It was launched on April 12, 2005.NRHM focuses on strengthening healthcare infrastructure in rural areas and improving the overall health of rural communities.

Atal Amrit Abhiyan:

This program, launched in Assam on December 25, 2016, provides comprehensive health insurance coverage to families living below the poverty line.

Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana

It was launched on April 1, 2008.Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana(RSBY) provides health insurance coverage for below-poverty-line (BPL) families and unorganized laborers.

National Tobacco Control Program

It was launched on May 31, 2008. Launched to curb the use of tobacco and its harmful effects, this program includes awareness campaigns, tobacco cessation services, and regulation of tobacco products.

National Mental Health Program

National Mental Health Program(NHMP) was launched in 1982 to provide mental health services, promote mental health awareness, and reduce the stigma associated with mental health issues.
